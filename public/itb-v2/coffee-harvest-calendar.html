Coffee Harvest Calendar by Origin

Knowing when each origin harvests and when it arrives at US importers is how you buy fresh crop and build a quality-first sourcing calendar.

Coffee harvest timing determines when fresh crop lots arrive at your importer and ultimately when you have access to peak-freshness green coffee. Building your buying calendar around harvest cycles is one of the highest-leverage things a specialty roaster can do — it's the difference between buying new crop at peak and buying past crop at discount pricing because it sat in a warehouse for 18 months.

This reference calendar covers the major specialty origins with peak harvest windows, typical importer arrival timing, and what to watch for in each region.

How to Use This Calendar

Three dates matter for each origin: harvest (when cherries are picked), milling and processing (when green coffee is produced), and importer arrival (when it lands at US importers and becomes available to roasters). The gap between harvest and your purchase is typically 3-6 months. The freshness window for green coffee begins at arrival and closes around 12-18 months later, depending on storage conditions.

Building Your Buying Calendar

For roasters who want to run a fully fresh-crop program: build a 12-month buying plan that maps each origin to its availability window and estimates your volume needs. You'll need to commit to certain lots 2-4 months before you need them as you get to know importer arrival timing. Ask your importer for arrival updates in their preferred channels (most US importers now use email newsletters or Cropster Trace for arrival alerts).

For roasters with tighter inventory budgets: prioritize your two or three signature origins and buy them on the freshest possible timing, rather than trying to stock 8 origins simultaneously. Fresh-crop single origin quality at your price point will win customer loyalty over volume variety.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is coffee harvested?
Coffee harvest timing depends on origin and hemisphere. In the Northern Hemisphere (Central America, Mexico, Ethiopia, Kenya), main harvest runs October through March. In the Southern Hemisphere (Brazil, Peru), harvest runs April through August. Colombia produces year-round due to its unique microclimates and mitaca (secondary) crop cycle.
When does new crop Guatemala coffee arrive?
Guatemala's main harvest runs October through February, with Huehuetenango extending to March. New crop lots typically arrive at US importers May through August, with the freshest lots available June-July.
What is new crop coffee?
New crop coffee refers to lots from the most recent harvest season. Green coffee degrades in quality over time, so new crop lots at peak freshness (within 6-12 months of harvest) are preferred by specialty roasters. Contrast with past crop (12-24 months post-harvest) or old crop (24+ months).
Which coffee origins are available year-round?
Colombia is the most notable year-round origin due to its main and mitaca (secondary) crop cycles and diverse microclimates. Some East African origins (particularly certain Ethiopian regions) also have extended availability. Most other origins have defined harvest windows with 6-12 month availability at importers.
