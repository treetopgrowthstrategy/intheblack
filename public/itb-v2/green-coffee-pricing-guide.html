Green coffee pricing is one of the most opaque parts of the specialty supply chain. C Market prices, differential pricing, importer margins, direct trade premiums, and lot-specific pricing all operate simultaneously without a centralized reference that roasters can rely on. This guide demystifies the pricing structure and gives you working benchmarks for 2026 green coffee buying.

Understanding the C Market

The "C Market" is the ICE Futures U.S. Arabica coffee futures contract, which sets a global commodity baseline price for standard-grade washed Arabica. It's quoted in cents per pound and fluctuates based on global supply, weather forecasts, currency movements, and speculative trading.

As of 2026, the C Market has traded in a range of 180-260 cents/lb after significant volatility over the prior three years. Current spot price is available at ICE Futures (https://www.theice.com/coffee) — check it before any buying conversation with an importer.

"The C Market is a floor, not a ceiling. Specialty coffee trades at a differential above C Market. Understanding that differential is how you understand whether you're paying a fair price."

Differentials: How Specialty Pricing Works

Specialty green coffee is priced as "C Market + differential." The differential is the premium above commodity price that reflects quality, origin, certifications, and lot exclusivity. Differentials are negotiated between origin exporters and importers, then passed through to roasters.

What Affects the Price You Pay

Volume: Buying full bags (roughly 60-70kg) vs. spot lots vs. container-level volume changes pricing significantly. Full bag pricing runs 10-20% below spot lot (sample box) pricing. Container pricing (300+ bags) is negotiated separately and typically offers the best per-pound rate.

Importer relationship: Established importers with long-term producer relationships often offer better prices than spot-market brokers. The trade-off: importers lock in contracts months in advance, limiting your flexibility to chase the best lots as they emerge.

Certification premiums: Fair Trade, Rainforest Alliance, and organic certifications add $0.15-$0.75/lb to base pricing. These certifications matter for your retail positioning and customer trust but represent real cost that must be factored into your pricing model.

Proximity to harvest: New crop lots at peak freshness (within 6-12 months of harvest) command higher prices than older crop. Past crop pricing can be 15-30% lower, but cup quality degrades — factor this into your quality standards.

Building a Green Coffee Budget

A working rule of thumb for specialty roasters: green coffee cost should represent 30-45% of your roasted retail price. At $18/bag retail, you have $5.40-$8.10/100g to cover green cost. At $30/bag retail for a micro-lot, your green cost budget expands to $9-$13.50/100g.

For a 60-70kg full bag purchase at $5/lb landed: your green cost is $661-$770. At a 15% roasting loss and $18/bag retail (227g bag), that's approximately 297 bags, or $5,346 revenue on a $770 green investment — a 6.9x green-to-retail multiplier. This math varies significantly by roast loss, bag size, and retail price point.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current price of green coffee?
Green coffee pricing is tied to the ICE C Market plus a quality differential. In 2026, C Market has traded 180-260 cents/lb. Specialty grade washed coffee typically adds $0.50-$2.50/lb differential, putting landed specialty pricing at $2.30-$5.10/lb for most roasters depending on origin and SCA score.
How much does specialty green coffee cost?
Specialty green coffee (85-90 SCA) typically runs $2.30-$5.10/lb landed in the US. Micro-lot and traceable lots (90+ SCA) run $4-$8+/lb. Geisha and competition-grade lots can exceed $10-$60/lb depending on origin and provenance.
