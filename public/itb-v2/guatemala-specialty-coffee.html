Guatemala Specialty Coffee: A Roaster's Regional Guide

Guatemala produces eight distinct coffee regions — each with a different elevation, climate, and cup profile. Here's how to source and position them.

Guatemala has produced specialty-grade coffee since the 1970s, when the ANACAFE grading system formalized what highland farmers had known for generations: the country's volcanic soil, altitude range, and microclimates produce coffees of exceptional quality. Today Guatemala's eight growing regions produce distinct cup profiles that rival any origin in the world.

For roasters, Guatemala represents a value proposition that's hard to match: high-altitude, complex, SCA 86-92 scoring coffees at price points significantly below comparable Africa or Panama lots. This guide covers the regions, what to expect in the cup, and how to source well.

Guatemala's Eight Coffee Regions

Huehuetenango

Guatemala's Premier Specialty Region

Huehuetenango produces Guatemala's most celebrated coffees. Elevations from 1,500-2,000m, dry mountain climate, and the Cuilco valley wind system create exceptional growing conditions. Cup profile: bright acidity, complex fruit, peach and apple, clean finish. This is the region where Geisha and other high-altitude varieties perform best. SHG classification (Strictly Hard Bean, grown above 4,500 feet) is the relevant quality designation here.

Antigua

The Classic Profile

Antigua is Guatemala's most internationally recognized coffee region, surrounded by three active volcanoes (Agua, Acatenango, Fuego). The volcanic soil contributes to a distinctive full body and mild acidity. Classic Antigua profile: chocolate, caramel, mild fruit, medium-high body. Consistent quality, widely available, excellent for espresso blends and consumer-accessible single origins.

Atitlán

Lake-Altitude Complex

Coffees grown on the slopes surrounding Lake Atitlán at 1,500-1,700m elevation. The lake moderates temperature and provides humidity that contributes to slow cherry development. Cup profile: pronounced acidity, grape and citrus notes, medium body. Atitlán coffees respond particularly well to light roasting, where the acidity shines.

Sourcing Guatemala: What to Know

Guatemala's harvest runs October through February in most regions, with Huehuetenango typically finishing January-March due to higher altitude and slower cherry development. New crop lots typically arrive at US importers May through August. Buying early-season (May-June arrival) gets you the freshest lots with the most competitive pricing from importers still clearing space from the previous season.

Look for SHG or SHB (Strictly Hard/Strictly Hard Bean) designation, which indicates growth above 4,500 feet. Below that designation, quality is more variable. Ask for the specific municipality or farm origin — regional claims are broad and quality within regions varies significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Guatemala specialty coffee known for?
Guatemala produces eight distinct regional coffee profiles. Huehuetenango is the most celebrated, known for bright acidity, complex fruit, and floral notes at elevations up to 2,000m. Antigua produces the most internationally recognized profile: full body, chocolate, and mild acidity from volcanic soil.
What is SHG coffee from Guatemala?
SHG stands for Strictly Hard Bean (or Strictly Hard Grown), a ANACAFE grading designation for coffee grown above approximately 4,500 feet (1,370m). At these elevations, beans develop more slowly, producing denser seeds with higher sugar content and more complex flavor compounds.
Is Guatemala coffee good for espresso?
Yes. Antigua Guatemala is particularly well-regarded for espresso and espresso blends: full body, chocolate and caramel notes, mild acidity, and excellent crema. Huehuetenango Guatemalas also work well as single-origin espresso for specialty-focused cafes.
When is Guatemala coffee harvested?
Guatemala's main harvest runs October through February, with higher-altitude regions like Huehuetenango finishing January through March. New crop lots typically reach US roasters May through August. Buying May-June arrival dates gets the freshest lots.
