Buying Geisha Coffee Beans: What Roasters Need to Know

A Geisha lot purchase can run thousands of dollars. This guide covers green grading, pricing benchmarks, quality red flags, and where to find reliable supply.

Sourcing Geisha coffee beans for your roastery is a decision with real financial stakes. A full lot of Panama Geisha from a top farm can run $5,000-$15,000 for a modest purchase. Even a 30-bag Guatemala Geisha buy represents a meaningful investment. This page covers what roasters need to know before placing an order: variety identification, green quality indicators, price benchmarks, and where to find reliable supply.

Identifying Genuine Geisha Beans (Green)

Geisha beans are visually distinctive in green form. Key identifiers:

CharacteristicGeishaStandard Arabica
Bean shapeElongated, pointed tipsOval, rounded
SizeLarge, often screen 17-18+Variable
Center cutPronounced, often wavyStraight to slightly curved
Color (green)Blue-green, vividPale green to yellow-green
DensityHigh (hard bean, high altitude)Variable

Note: visual identification alone is insufficient. Genotyping is the only definitive method. Request cup scores and farm documentation from any supplier selling Geisha at premium prices.

Green Grading and What to Ask For

When evaluating a Geisha green lot, ask your importer or broker for:

  • SCA cupping score from a licensed Q Grader (85+ minimum for specialty; 88+ for Geisha premium positioning)
  • Screen size report (size 17+ preferred for washed Geisha)
  • Defect count (Category 1 and 2 defect totals per 350g sample)
  • Moisture content (10.5-12.5% optimal; outside this range indicates storage or processing issues)
  • Water activity (0.55-0.65 Aw; above 0.65 indicates mold risk)
  • Processing documentation (washed, natural, honey, anaerobic — and how the anaerobic was done)
  • Farm elevation (below 1,500m is a red flag for Geisha variety expression)

Where to Buy Geisha Coffee Beans Green

Importers with consistent Geisha supply include Trabocca (Netherlands/US), Cafe Imports (Minneapolis), Osito Coffee (San Francisco), and Atlas Coffee Importers. For smaller volumes and direct-from-origin Guatemala Geisha, In The Black Coffee Co. sources from El Quetzal Coffee Co.'s Guatemalan highland farms.

For Panama lots, the Best of Panama auction (held annually, results available from SCA/BOP websites) is the price benchmark. Lots not clearing the auction are often available directly from farms at below-auction prices — worth tracking down through importers with Panama farm relationships.

How Much Geisha to Buy

Buy what you can sell at a premium price point within 6 months of roasting. Geisha's delicate compounds degrade faster than most Arabica varieties once roasted. As green, properly stored (climate-controlled, dark, sealed in GrainPro), Geisha holds quality for 12-18 months post-harvest.

For a new Geisha program: start with a single 30-bag (roughly 1,800kg) lot. Test at retail and wholesale before committing to larger volumes. Oversupply is a bigger problem in Geisha than with standard origins because retail price resistance above $45-$60/bag limits your sell-through rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I buy Geisha coffee beans for my roastery?
Contact specialty importers (Cafe Imports, Trabocca, Atlas) or brokers like In The Black Coffee Co. for Guatemalan highland Geisha. Always request a cupping score from a Q Grader and green sample before committing to a full lot purchase.
What is a fair price for Geisha coffee beans?
Fair price depends on origin and quality. Guatemala Geisha runs $10-$30/lb green. Panama Geisha from top farms runs $30-$120/lb. Any Geisha priced below $8/lb should be evaluated critically — correct terroir and processing is expensive at origin.
How long do Geisha green coffee beans last?
Properly stored in GrainPro bags in a climate-controlled (65-70°F), dark environment, Geisha green beans maintain quality for 12-18 months post-harvest. Geisha's delicate aromatic compounds degrade faster than standard Arabica varieties.
What screen size are Geisha coffee beans?
High-quality Geisha typically screens at size 17-18+ due to the variety's characteristically large, elongated bean structure. Smaller screen sizes in a Geisha lot may indicate lower altitude growing conditions or variety admixture.
