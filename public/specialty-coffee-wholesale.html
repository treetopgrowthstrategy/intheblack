Commodity vs Specialty

What makes it specialty wholesale

Commodity coffee wholesale is traded by price: standard grades, futures contracts, blending for consistency. Specialty wholesale trades on quality, traceability, and story:

The Supply Chain

How specialty coffee gets to your roastery

A specialty green lot's journey:

  1. Farm: Cherries are harvested selectively (usually hand-picked for specialty). Processing occurs at the farm or at a centralized wet mill. The farm's altitude, variety selection, and processing method determine quality ceiling.
  2. Exporter: An in-country exporter (sometimes the farm itself, often a specialized exporter like El Quetzal Coffee Co.) prepares the lot for export — milling, sorting, quality control, documentation, bagging (60kg or 69kg jute/GrainPro bags).
  3. Importer: The importer handles logistics (ocean freight, customs clearance, fumigation if required, US warehouse receipt), provides credit terms, and offers the lot to roaster buyers through their sales team or broker network.
  4. Broker/Agency: Optional intermediary who markets specific lots to roasters on behalf of importers or exporters. Commission-based; doesn't take physical possession of the coffee.
  5. Roaster: Receives the green coffee, roasts to profile, and sells to wholesale cafe accounts or direct-to-consumer channels.
Quality and Grading

How specialty coffee is graded

The SCA (Specialty Coffee Association) cupping protocol grades coffee on a 100-point scale across 10 attributes, each scored 6–10. Total possible points: 100. Specialty coffee requires 80+.

  • 80–84: Specialty. Good quality, regional character, suitable for house blends and volume single origins.
  • 85–89: Excellent. Distinctive cup, clear origin character. Strong candidates for featured single origins.
  • 90+: Exceptional. Extraordinary complexity and clarity. Competition-grade. Very limited supply and premium pricing.

Physical green grading: Screen size (larger generally indicates altitude and density), moisture content (10–12% optimal), defect count (specialty requires zero Category 1 defects, max 5 Category 2 per 300g sample), and water activity.

Relationships

Building importer and farm relationships

In specialty wholesale, relationship quality determines supply quality. The roasters who get first access to the best lots, locked pricing on their favorites, and advance notification of exceptional micro-lots are the ones who have invested in genuine partnerships with their importers.

Relationship-building practices that work:

  • Annual visits to importer offices and cupping events
  • Consistently paying invoices on time (a basic commercial signal of reliability)
  • Providing specific, useful feedback after cupping samples
  • Committing volume when you find a lot you love, not hedging on order size
  • Origin trips when budget and timeline allow — direct farm visits build relationships that persist

Frequently Asked Questions

What is specialty coffee wholesale?
Specialty wholesale coffee is green or roasted coffee meeting SCA specialty grade (80+ score, zero category-1 defects) traded with farm-level traceability. It commands a price differential above commodity grade that reflects quality, origin transparency, and often direct or fair trade practices. It's traded through specialty importers, brokers, and direct relationships rather than commodity exchanges.
How is specialty coffee wholesale different from commodity coffee?
Commodity coffee trades on price and grade with minimal traceability. Specialty trades on quality score, farm provenance, variety, processing method, and story. Specialty pricing includes a differential above the C market to compensate for quality and traceability. Specialty wholesale relationships are longer-term and partnership-oriented; commodity relationships are typically transactional.
How do specialty coffee importers work?
Importers source green coffee from producing countries, handle logistics and customs clearance, warehouse in consuming markets, and sell to roasters. They provide quality documentation (cupping scores, farm info, processing notes), offer credit terms, and maintain supply chain relationships that individual roasters can't replicate. Most specialty roasters work with 2–4 importers for different origins.
What is a coffee broker?
A coffee broker or agency intermediates between importers/exporters and roasters, typically on a commission basis. Unlike importers, brokers don't take physical possession of the coffee. They match buyers with available lots based on quality requirements and origin preferences. What are typical wholesale coffee minimum order quantities?
Importers typically have 1–5 bag minimums for established relationships (60kg bags = 132 lbs). New accounts may have higher minimums. Some importers offer spot samples in 1kg quantities before larger orders. How do I find specialty coffee wholesale suppliers?
Primary channels: (1) SCA Expo and World of Coffee importer cupping tables; (2) broker and agency introductions; (3) online platforms (Algrano, Sucafina) for direct sourcing; (4) referrals from other specialty roasters (the specialty community is collaborative). In The Black Coffee Co. offers introductions to Guatemalan specialty supply for roasters looking to build or diversify their Central American sourcing.
What is the difference between green coffee and roasted coffee wholesale?
Green coffee wholesale is the pre-roast supply chain — importers and brokers selling raw green beans to roasters in bags. Roasted coffee wholesale is roasters selling finished roasted coffee to cafes and retail accounts. Most specialty roasters buy green and sell roasted. Some wholesale green coffee traders also deal roasted, but they're separate market segments.
How does payment work for wholesale green coffee?
New buyers typically start with prepayment or short terms (7–14 days). Established accounts receive 30–60 day payment terms from importers. Some importers offer financing or consignment arrangements for high-volume buyers. All green coffee contracts specify payment terms, delivery terms (usually FOB or DDP US warehouse), and quality acceptance terms.
